Exercise Demonstration

Barbell Squat

Compound Strength Legs

The barbell squat is a compound exercise that primarily targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. It also engages the core and lower back muscles for stabilization.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1 Starting Position: Set up a barbell on a squat rack at approximately shoulder height. Step under the bar and position it across the back of your shoulders (not on your neck).
2 Unrack: Stand up to lift the bar off the rack, taking a step back. Position your feet shoulder-width apart with toes slightly pointed outward.
3 Descent: Keeping your chest up and back straight, bend your knees and hips to lower your body as if sitting in a chair. Aim to get your thighs parallel to the ground.
4 Ascent: Push through your heels to stand back up to the starting position, keeping your back straight and chest up throughout the movement.
5 Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions before carefully re-racking the barbell.
Pro Tip

Keep your heels firmly planted on the ground throughout the movement. If you have trouble with this, try placing small weight plates under your heels or use weightlifting shoes with a raised heel.

Common Mistakes
Knees Caving Inward

Keep your knees tracking over your toes throughout the movement to protect your knee joints and engage the correct muscles.

Rounding the Back

Maintain a neutral spine position. Rounding your back can lead to injuries, especially under load.

Rising Onto Toes

Keep your full foot in contact with the ground. Lifting your heels reduces stability and can strain your knees.
