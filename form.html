Barbell Squat

Compound Legs Barbell

(Image Placeholder)
Quick Tips
  • Keep your chest up and back straight throughout the movement
  • Push through your heels, not your toes
  • Knees should track in line with your toes
  • Aim for at least parallel depth (thighs parallel to ground)
  • Breathe in before descending, brace your core, and exhale on the way up

Proper Form Guide

1
Setup

Position the barbell on a rack at approximately shoulder height. Step under the bar so that it rests on your upper back across your traps (not on your neck). Grip the bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Unrack the bar by straightening your legs and step back from the rack.

2
Starting Position

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider, toes pointed slightly outward. Keep your chest up, back straight, and core tight. Fix your gaze at a point on the wall slightly above eye level to maintain a neutral spine position.

3
Descent

Initiate the movement by bending at the hips and knees simultaneously, as if sitting back into a chair. Keep your knees tracking over your toes (not caving inward). Maintain the natural arch in your lower back as you descend until your thighs are at least parallel to the ground.

4
Bottom Position

At the bottom of the squat, your knees should be bent at approximately 90 degrees. Your weight should be distributed through your heels and midfoot, not shifted forward to your toes. Keep your chest up and core tight.

5
Ascent

Drive through your heels to push yourself back up, extending your hips and knees simultaneously. Keep your back straight and chest up throughout the movement. Continue until your legs are fully extended, returning to the starting position.

Common Mistakes
  • Knees caving inward - Ensure knees track in line with toes by focusing on pushing knees outward during the movement.
  • Rising onto toes - Keep weight in heels and midfoot; you should be able to wiggle your toes at the bottom of the squat.
  • Rounding the back - Maintain a neutral spine position by keeping chest up and core engaged.
  • Not reaching proper depth - Aim to get thighs at least parallel to the ground for full muscle engagement.
  • Letting the bar path drift forward - The bar should travel in a vertical line over mid-foot throughout the movement.
Muscles Worked

  • Primary: Quadriceps, Glutes
  • Secondary: Hamstrings, Lower Back, Core
  • Tertiary: Calves, Upper Back
Variations
  • Front Squat - Places more emphasis on quadriceps.
  • Goblet Squat - Great for beginners to learn proper form.
  • Bulgarian Split Squat - Single-leg variation for balance and unilateral strength.
  • Overhead Squat - Advanced variation that increases shoulder and core engagement.
  • Box Squat - Helps develop power from the bottom position.

Conventional Deadlift

Compound Back Legs Barbell

(Image Placeholder)

(Image Placeholder)
Quick Tips
  • Keep the bar close to your body throughout the movement
  • Maintain a neutral spine position from setup to lockout
  • Drive through your heels and push the floor away
  • Engage your lats by thinking about "protecting your armpits"
  • Breathe and brace before initiating the lift

Proper Form Guide

1
Setup

Position yourself with the barbell over your mid-foot. Your feet should be hip-width apart, with toes pointing slightly outward. The barbell should be about 1 inch from your shins.

2
Grip

Bend at the hips to reach down and grip the bar just outside your legs. You can use either a double overhand grip (both palms facing you) or a mixed grip (one palm facing you, one facing away) for heavier weights. Your arms should be straight, acting as ropes that connect your shoulders to the bar.

3
Starting Position

Lower your hips and raise your chest to create tension in your body. Your shoulders should be slightly in front of the bar, with your back flat (neutral spine). Engage your lats by pulling your shoulders back and down. Take a deep breath, brace your core, and prepare to lift.

4
The Pull

Initiate the pull by pushing through your heels and driving your hips forward. The bar should travel in a straight vertical path, staying close to your body throughout the movement. Your knees and hips should extend simultaneously.

5
Lockout

At the top of the movement, stand tall with your shoulders back, chest up, and hips fully extended. Do not hyperextend your lower back. Hold this position briefly to complete the lift.

6
Lowering the Weight

To lower the weight, hinge at the hips first, then bend your knees once the bar passes them. Keep the bar close to your body and maintain control throughout the descent until the weight returns to the floor.

Common Mistakes
  • Rounding the back - Maintain a neutral spine throughout the lift to prevent injury.
  • Starting with hips too low - This turns the movement into a squat pattern rather than a hip hinge.
  • Allowing the bar to drift away from the body - Keep the bar close to your shins and thighs throughout the movement.
  • Jerking the weight off the floor - Develop tension before lifting and pull the weight smoothly.
  • Hyperextending at the top - Simply stand tall with hips fully extended; don't lean back excessively.
